Plot

The story begins in Paris, where Regina Lampert (played by Audrey Hepburn) is contemplating divorcing her secretive husband, Charles Lampert. However, her plans take an unexpected turn when she returns home from a skiing trip to find her husband murdered, and she becomes entangled in a web of intrigue.

Cast and Crew

Audrey Hepburn the epitome of grace and elegance, takes on the role of Regina Lampert, a young widow caught in a web of deception and intrigue. Known for her iconic style and impeccable acting, Hepburn's performance in Charade showcases her talent as a leading lady in the Golden Age of Hollywood.

Cary Grant the suave and debonair leading man of his era, plays the mysterious and charismatic Peter Joshua. His impeccable comedic timing and effortless charm make him the perfect foil to Hepburn's character, creating a delightful on-screen chemistry that's unforgettable.

Stanley Donen the visionary director behind this film, brings a blend of suspense and humor to the story, creating a cinematic experience that keeps you on the edge of your seat while making you laugh out loud. His direction in Charade is a testament to his filmmaking genius.

Reviews

Roger Ebert, Chicago Sun-Times: "Charade" is a gem of a movie, a delightful blend of suspense and humor, carried by the irresistible charm of Audrey Hepburn and Cary Grant. Stanley Donen's direction creates an atmosphere of constant intrigue and witty banter that keeps you engaged from start to finish. It's a classic for all the right reasons.

The New York Times: "Audrey Hepburn and Cary Grant make an enchanting pair in 'Charade.' The film's plot is a rollercoaster of twists and turns, and the chemistry between the two leads is nothing short of electric. It's a sophisticated and timeless thriller with a touch of romance that remains a cinematic treasure."
